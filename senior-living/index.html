Start broad, then narrow

The site is organized to help families move from a broad market view into more local pages and, eventually, specific community details. That makes the browsing experience easier to follow and easier to expand market by market.

State pages

State pages introduce the markets currently live within a state and create a clean structure for future expansion.

Area pages

Area pages group the parts of a metro or region that families commonly compare together before narrowing to a city or neighborhood.

City and community pages

City pages help families browse a manageable local list. Community pages go deeper into care types, scores, and comparison support.

Coverage grows market by market

Compass Place is built to expand over time, but the public web structure is intentionally focused on markets that are actually live. That keeps the site more useful for families and gives each branch a clearer local identity.

As new markets are added, this same structure can extend cleanly into new state, area, city, and community pages without changing the logic of the site.
